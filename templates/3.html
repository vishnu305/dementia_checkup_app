Introduction about Dementia Disease

Dementia is basically a syndrome which usually remains lifelong and show the nature of progression in terms of symptoms as the affected person starts ageing. It is not a disease but syndrome which is a term used to refer to the group of symptoms which do not have any fixed treatment. In this disease, a person loses his cognitive functioning such as ability to think, memory, behaviour and the ability to perform day to day activities. It usually affects older people and it is abnormal part of ageing.

Alzheimer’s disease falls under the category of Dementia and it is one of the major cause for 60-70% of the cases reported every year. Dementia is responsible for disability and dependency of older people on others. People with dementia not only experience these symptoms but also have physical, psychological, societal and economic impact on their career, families and society. All the mental diseases which comes under the broad term ‘Dementia’ occur due to changes taking place in the brain. These changes lead to decrease in thinking skills of people also known as cognitive abilities which can severely affect daily life of people and their day-to-day activities. It also affects relationships, behaviour and feelings. People in this era are not having enough awareness and understanding about Dementia which often leads to delay in the care and diagnosis of the patient. Hence there is a crucial need for spreading awareness and ensuring complete understanding of the Disease which often occurs in old aged people.
